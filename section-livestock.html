Labor reality at 8 coops: 8 coops × ~20 minutes per coop per day (coop move + fermented feed + water + eggs) = 2.67 hrs/day, 365 days per year = ~973 hrs/year. This is the largest single daily time commitment on the farm — more than any garden task. It cannot be deferred or batched. Build your daily routine around it before acquiring more coops than you can sustain solo.
Brahmas lay through winter when most breeds stop — consistent revenue November–February. They forage actively behind the sheep rotation, consuming barber pole larvae, beetles, grubs, and earthworms. This is the farm's primary fly control mechanism — no products needed. At culling (Year 4), Brahma stew hens sell at $13–18 each due to their size and flavor. Autumn molt (Sep–Nov) = ~35 days no eggs; this coincides with peak root harvest revenue, which absorbs the gap.

How to ferment: Add feed to 5-gal food-grade bucket. Cover with water (2:1 water-to-feed ratio by weight). Stir. Loosely cover (needs air exchange). Leave at room temp 48–72 hrs until slightly sour-smelling and bubbling. Feed daily; start next batch same day you feed. Fermented liquid from the bottom can be diluted and given as drinking water. Reduces feed cost, improves gut health, typically increases production 5–10%.
