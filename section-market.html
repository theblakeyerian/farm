Revenue split follows the master plan: 65% direct market, 35% restaurant wholesale. The restaurant channel is higher margin (no booth time, bulk packing, standing orders) but requires reliability above all else. One missed delivery drops a restaurant account. The market channel has lower per-hour return but builds brand recognition, drives flower sales that increase all other sales 20–30%, and gives direct customer feedback that shapes what to grow more of. Both channels are essential and reinforce each other.

Restaurant account rules: (1) Never promise what you can't deliver — one missed order loses the account. (2) Communicate shortfalls 48 hours early, not day-of. (3) Standardize your pack — restaurants need consistent pack weights, not "about a pound." (4) Invoice on delivery, net 14 days. Use a simple Google Sheet invoice template until you hit 4+ accounts, then consider FarmersWeb or Barn2Door for order management. (5) Your first 2 accounts should be restaurants you can personally visit — face-to-face relationships hold when supply gets tight.

5.7% of gross revenue in market and sales costs is extremely efficient for direct farm sales. The industry average for CSA and farmers market operations is 8–15%. You benefit from low booth fees in a rural market, no middleman, and the integrated chicken egg carton cost being partially offset by the $8/dozen price point.
