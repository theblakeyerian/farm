The real case for perennial priority — 5× revenue per hour by Year 5
The 2× weighting in the demand index understates the advantage. Perennials generate $367/hr vs. $72/hr for annuals — a true 5× labor efficiency advantage at maturity. This ratio improves every year as the perennial system becomes more self-sustaining (comfrey replacing purchased inputs, crowns expanding without replanting, soil biology deepening). By Year 10, the perennial plots are generating roughly the same revenue from perhaps 80 hours of labor per year — pushing the ratio toward $700+/hr. The annual garden always requires the same 780 hours regardless of year.

Practical implication: In any year where labor is the binding constraint (which it is — see the 272-hr March peak), the perennial system generates revenue with far less of it. Establishing all four perennial plots completely and correctly in Year 0 is the highest-ROI investment on the farm.
